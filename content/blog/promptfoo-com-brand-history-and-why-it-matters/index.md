## The Name "Promptfoo": Where It Comes From

The name **promptfoo** is a portmanteau that any programmer instantly recognizes:

- **Prompt**: The input you give to an AI model — the text, context, and instructions that shape the AI's response
- **Foo**: The universal programming placeholder, from the classic trio `foo`, `bar`, `baz` — used in examples since the earliest days of computing

Put them together and you get **promptfoo**: the placeholder for AI prompts, a testing framework where prompts are the thing being tested, evaluated, and validated.

The name is memorable because it sits at the intersection of two worlds: the ancient tradition of `foo` as programmer shorthand, and the new discipline of prompt engineering. When developers hear "promptfoo," they immediately understand — this is about testing prompts the way you test code.

## The Domain History

**promptfoo.com** was established as a resource hub for the AI prompt testing community during the early years of the LLM application boom. As developers began building serious applications on top of GPT-3, then GPT-4, then Claude and Gemini, the need for systematic prompt testing became clear — and promptfoo.com emerged as a home for that conversation.

The companion domain **promptfu.com** (note: no second "o") is the sister site — a tech knowledge base and blog covering command-line tools, AI development, and prompt engineering. Together, these domains form a cluster of authority in the prompt engineering and AI testing space.

We also own both domains, which positions promptfoo.com uniquely: it has the exact-match domain for the most-searched term in AI testing — `promptfoo` — while promptfu.com provides the deep content library that signals domain expertise to both readers and search engines.

## Why Exact-Match Domains Still Matter in 2026

There's a longstanding debate in SEO about whether exact-match domains (EMDs) carry meaningful weight. The short answer: **yes, for navigational and branded queries, they matter enormously**.

When a developer types `promptfoo` into Google, they may be looking for:

1. The npm package
2. The GitHub repository
3. Documentation
4. Tutorials and guides
5. The company that built it

For queries 4 and 5 — resources and context — **promptfoo.com is the natural destination**. An exact-match domain signals to both users and search algorithms that this is an authoritative source for "promptfoo" as a concept, not just a specific implementation.

This is especially valuable now that promptfoo.dev has been acquired by OpenAI. The .dev acquisition means corporate stewardship of the framework; the .com remains the independent, community-oriented brand authority.
